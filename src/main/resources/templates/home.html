Frequently Asked Questions

Premiums are calculated based on factors such as the type of coverage, the amount of coverage, the policyholder's age, health, and lifestyle, and the risk level associated with insuring the individual or asset.

A deductible is the amount the policyholder must pay out of pocket before the insurance company starts covering the expenses. Higher deductibles generally mean lower premiums and vice versa.

To file a claim, contact your insurance company as soon as possible after the incident occurs. Provide all necessary documentation and information required to process your claim, such as accident reports, medical bills, or repair estimates.
